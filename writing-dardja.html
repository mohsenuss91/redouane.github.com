Introducing Dardja

People in algeria speak at the very least two langauges, the local arabic dialect, known as dardja, and french

the darjda receipe : how to cook a new language

1 - first you need a traditional berber plate, you can get one of those anywhere in algeria 2 - Wait for the arabs to come and bring their tradition, language, ideology, religion, camels, food (and more!) 3 - throw in some spanish and turkish words 4 - mix everything in a pressure cooker and let it boil for a couple of years 5 - wait for the french to come and invade your country 6 - open the pressure cooker, throw in some french words 7 - wait for some new word to get popular enough, and throw it in the mix, then iterate. 8 - done, you've cooked dardja, bon appetit !

it is not an officially recognized language, yet it is spoken daily by the vast majority of algerians
